# googie

Swooping rooflines reaching toward the stars, boomerang canopies defying gravity, and neon signs blazing against twilight skies — the jet-age optimism of 1950s American roadside architecture. This is the coffee shop on the highway to the future, where every building looks ready to launch into orbit, with starburst decorations, massive plate glass windows, and pylons angled like rocket fins slicing through the suburban night.

## Color Palette

- Primary: Turquoise (#40E0D0), hot coral/salmon (#FF6B6B), chrome silver (#C0C0C0), cream white (#FFF8E7)
- Background: Deep twilight blue (#1A1A2E), warm sunset gradient (coral to purple), or clean cream — never harsh white
- Accents: Atomic orange (#FF5722), mint green (#98FF98), electric yellow (#FFE135), bubblegum pink (#FF69B4), neon red (#FF3131)
- Text: Cream or white on dark backgrounds; charcoal on light; chrome-effect for headlines

## Visual Elements

- **Swept rooflines and canopies**: Dramatic upward-curving roof shapes with sharp points at the ends, like a seagull's wings or a flying saucer about to take off — the defining Googie silhouette
- **Angled pylons and supports**: Structural columns tilted at dramatic angles (never vertical), tapering to points, painted in contrasting colors — buildings that look like they're leaning into the future
- **Boomerang and parabola shapes**: Asymmetric curved forms used for signs, counters, and decorative elements — the boomerang is to Googie what the column is to Greek temples
- **Starbursts and asterisks**: Multi-pointed star shapes (8-12 points) decorating everything from wall clocks to sign decorations to pattern accents — atomic sparkle everywhere
- **Neon tube signage**: Glowing tubular letterforms and arrow shapes, with visible glow halos and color bleeding at night — signs that can be read from a moving car at 60mph
- **Massive plate glass windows**: Large angular window walls divided by thin mullions into geometric patterns — interior and exterior flowing together
- **Chrome and polished metal**: Reflective silver surfaces on stools, counters, trim, and structural elements — the machine-age sheen of the future
- **Kidney and amoeba shapes**: Organic freeform blob shapes for pools, planters, coffee tables — biomorphic counterpoint to the sharp angles
- **Zigzag and chevron patterns**: Angular decorative borders, folded-plate textures, zigzag rooflines — serrated energy lines
- **Flying saucer and orbital motifs**: Disc shapes, Saturn-ring details, antenna spires — Space Age iconography made architectural
- **Bold arrow shapes**: Directional pointers integrated into signs and architecture, promising speed and forward motion
- **Terrazzo and aggregate textures**: Speckled surfaces suggesting space-age materials, embedded chips of color in neutral grounds

## Typography

- Bold sans-serif with exaggerated geometry — letters stretched, angled, or swooped to match architectural forms
- Neon tube lettering: letterforms that look like they're made of bent glass tubes, with characteristic neon glow and rounded tube corners
- Titles at dramatic angles, following the sweep of a roofline (10-20 degree tilts)
- Mixed case for approachability, but key words can be ALL CAPS for signage impact
- Script and brush lettering for accent words — the "Coffee Shop" in cursive beneath the "BOB'S" in block letters
- Chrome-effect or dimensional lettering for headlines — letters that pop forward with highlight and shadow
- NO serif fonts, NO blackletter, NO condensed modern fonts — this is bold, confident, mid-century signage

## Anti-Patterns

- **NOT Art Deco**: Googie is optimistic and horizontal where Art Deco is formal and vertical. No gold, no ziggurats, no symmetrical fountain shapes. Googie swoops; Deco steps.
- **NOT generic atomic-age/mid-century modern**: While related, Googie is specifically *architectural* — swept roofs, angled pylons, neon signs. Not just starbursts on a cream background. The building silhouette must dominate.
- **NOT realistic photography or vintage filters**: This is stylized illustration of Googie architecture, not a sepia photograph. Bold colors, clean shapes, illustrated.
- **NOT Vegas-kitschy or overly tacky**: Googie has genuine optimism and confidence. Avoid ironic or campy treatment — play it straight.
- **NOT daytime-only**: Much of Googie's power comes from neon at night. Don't default to bright daylight scenes — twilight and night shots with glowing signs are essential.
- **NOT corporate flat illustration**: This needs actual architectural form — perspective, structure, shadow. The building is the star, not generic icons.
- **NO scattered floating elements**: Elements should be *anchored* to architectural structures (pylons, canopies, signs), not randomly floating in space.
