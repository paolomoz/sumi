# kandinsky

Bold geometric abstraction as visual music — large commanding circles with luminous colored halos floating against warm cream grounds, precise thin lines shooting diagonally across the canvas with mathematical tension, overlapping translucent planes creating mysterious depth. Deep saturated colors — ultramarine blue, cadmium yellow, vermillion red — in dynamic conversation with soft pinks, teals, and violets. This is Kandinsky's Composition VIII, Several Circles, Yellow-Red-Blue — pure geometric forms arranged with the rhythm and harmony of a symphony, each shape a musical note in a visual composition of spiritual resonance.

## Color Palette

- Primary: Ultramarine blue (#1D3C8C), cadmium yellow (#F7C621), vermillion red (#D04230)
- Extended: Deep violet (#5B3870), soft pink (#D98BA3), teal (#2D8E8E), burnt orange (#C96A34), olive green (#6B7346)
- Background: Warm cream (#F2E8D0), aged parchment (#E6D9C0), soft ochre wash (#E4D4A8) — never cold white
- Accents: Colored halos and glowing auras around circles — pink glow around black, blue corona around yellow, violet haze around red
- Darks: Dense velvety black (#1A1A1A) for bold circles and anchoring forms

### Palette Combinations

| Combination | Colors |

## Visual Elements

- **Large commanding circles with colored halos** — the signature Kandinsky element: bold circles (often black, blue, or red) surrounded by soft glowing auras in contrasting colors (pink around black, yellow around blue)
- **Thin precise diagonal lines** — single lines or parallel groups of 2-3 shooting across the composition at dramatic angles, creating tension and directional energy
- **Overlapping translucent planes** — geometric shapes (triangles, rectangles, irregular polygons) layered so colors blend and create spatial depth where they intersect
- **Concentric circle formations** — circles nested within circles, ripples radiating outward, target-like bull's eye patterns
- **Arcs and curves in tension with angles** — sweeping curved lines playing against sharp straight diagonals, the dynamism of opposition
- **Checkerboard fragments** — small grid patterns appearing as textural accents within the larger composition
- **Warm cream or parchment grounds** — never sterile white; backgrounds have subtle tonal warmth and variation
- **Dense velvety blacks** — heavy black circles, bold black lines, providing maximum contrast against luminous colors
- **Small geometric accents** — tiny triangles, circles, semicircles placed at line intersections or scattered as rhythmic punctuation
- **Colored arrow points** — small triangular directional accents, usually at the ends of lines or indicating movement
- **Soft gradient halos** — circles don't have hard edges; they glow outward into their colored auras
- **Parallel line groupings** — sets of 2-4 very fine lines running together, then diverging, creating texture and rhythm

## Compositional Patterns

| Content Structure | Composition | Reference |
|-------------------|-------------|-----------|
| Hierarchy/levels | Large dominant circle with glowing halo at upper left, smaller circles and geometric forms cascading below and rightward along diagonal lines | Composition VIII — commanding black circle anchoring the upper composition |
| Flow/process | Sweeping diagonal thrust from lower-left to upper-right, geometric forms arranged along the movement axis, arcs marking transitions | The eye carried along a diagonal trajectory of forms |
| Connections | Fine thin lines connecting circles of different colors and sizes, creating a constellation web of relationships across the composition | Network of precise lines linking floating geometric forms |
| Cycles | Concentric circles within a containing circle, overlapping arcs rotating around a center point, planetary orbits | Circles in a Circle — contained rotational energy |
| Comparison | Bold vertical division with contrasting color temperatures — warm saturated left vs. cool deep right, mirrored forms on each side | Yellow-Red-Blue's triptych color structure |
| Categories | Distinct geometric forms (circle, triangle, rectangle) each in their own saturated color, arranged in dynamic spatial relationship | Basic forms expressing different qualities through color-shape pairing |
| Growth | Expanding concentric rings with increasingly luminous halos, small to large progression along a diagonal axis | Circles growing outward like ripples, each ring a brighter halo |
| Networks | Grid of intersecting diagonal lines with colored circles at crossing points, each node glowing with its own aura | Composition VIII's field of geometric nodes connected by precise lines |
| Balance | Asymmetric equilibrium — heavy dark forms on one side balanced by lighter, more numerous forms on the other | Visual weight distributed through color intensity and size |
| Depth | Overlapping translucent planes creating layers, circles appearing to float at different distances | Cosmic space with forms at multiple z-depths |

## Typography

- Bold geometric sans-serif with architectural weight and confidence
- Lowercase preference — modern, clean, approachable
- Type as bold compositional element — large titles integrated into the geometric field
- Text can sit on diagonal angles following the composition's dynamic energy
- Key words highlighted in saturated primary colors against cream or contrasting fields
- Lettering can overlap with geometric forms, partially occluded for artistic depth
- No serif or decorative faces — pure geometric letterforms only
- Headlines can span full width with generous letter-spacing
- Numbers rendered with the same geometric precision as the visual forms

## Anti-Patterns

- **NOT Bauhaus poster style** — while Kandinsky taught at Bauhaus, his personal work is more spiritual and musical than the functional Bauhaus design aesthetic. Avoid flat poster graphics.
- **NOT chaotic or random** — Kandinsky's compositions are precisely calculated, every element placed with intention. Random floating shapes lack his musical structure.
- **NOT Klee** — Klee's work is whimsical, hand-drawn, with thin lines and muted dusty colors. Kandinsky is bolder, more geometric, with saturated colors and commanding presence.
- **NOT Mondrian** — Mondrian uses rigid grids and only primary colors plus black/white. Kandinsky uses free-floating forms, curves, and an extended color palette.
- **NOT cold or sterile** — despite geometric precision, Kandinsky's work has warmth: cream backgrounds, soft halos, rich saturated colors. Cold clinical graphics are wrong.
- **NOT illustration or pictorial** — Kandinsky is pure abstraction. Recognizable objects, figures, or scenes contradict the spiritual abstract vision.
- **NOT low contrast or muddy** — colors should be rich and saturated, blacks should be dense, halos should glow. Weak washed-out palettes lack Kandinsky's power.
- **NOT busy with small details** — compositions should have commanding large forms, not cluttered with tiny scattered elements.
- This must look like Kandinsky's Bauhaus-era abstractions — bold floating circles with luminous halos, precise diagonal lines, overlapping translucent planes, warm cream grounds, saturated primary and extended colors — not a modern geometric design exercise.
