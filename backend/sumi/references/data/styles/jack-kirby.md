# jack-kirby

Explosive cosmic superhero art channeling the "King of Comics" — massive squared-off figures with fists like cinder blocks punching through crackling energy fields, impossible foreshortening that shoves a boot or gauntlet right into your face, and swirling galaxies of black dots (Kirby Krackle) erupting from every cosmic portal. This is the Fourth World, the Celestials towering above Earth, Galactus consuming planets — technology rendered as impossible cathedral-machines bristling with pipes and circuits. Every panel feels like the universe is about to crack open.

## Color Palette

- Primary: Cosmic blue (#1A3A8F), Kirby magenta-pink (#E83E8C), power yellow (#FFD700), deep space black (#0A0A0A)
- Background: Inky black (#000000) for cosmic scenes, hot orange-red (#FF4500) for action explosions, deep indigo (#1B0A3C) for space vistas
- Accents: Kirby green (#3CB371), cosmic violet (#8B00FF), blast white (#FFFFFF), molten orange (#FF6600), energy cyan (#00E5FF)
- Halftone dots: Visible Ben-Day dots in magenta, cyan, and yellow — the cheap newsprint color separation that gives vintage comics their gritty texture
- Text: White reversed out of black panels, yellow on black for cosmic captions, black bold on white for impact

## Visual Elements

- **Kirby Krackle** — clusters of black dots/circles of varying sizes forming crescent-shaped energy fields, cosmic portals, and power effects. THE defining Kirby visual signature. Dense clusters of irregular black circles floating in space, arranged in loose arcs and crescents, with white or colored gaps between them suggesting pure cosmic energy. Not uniform — bigger dots mixed with smaller ones, denser at the edges of energy fields
- **Extreme foreshortening** — fists, boots, and weapons thrust at the viewer so aggressively that a single hand can be larger than the entire torso behind it. Camera angles from below looking up at colossal figures, or directly facing an incoming punch
- **Squared-off anatomy** — massive barrel chests, shoulders like concrete blocks, fingers like steel girders, jaws like anvils. Not the sleek anatomy of later artists — chunky, solid, monumental, as if carved from granite
- **Kirby tech-detail** — intricate alien machinery rendered with clusters of small circles, parallel lines, nested rectangles, riveted panels, and glowing nodes. Every surface of alien technology covered in obsessive geometric detail patterns that suggest function without being literally readable
- **Speed lines and impact bursts** — thick radiating lines from impact points, debris chunks flying outward, starburst explosions with jagged edges, concentric force rings around punches
- **Cosmic scale** — tiny planets and stars in the background establishing that the action happens at universal scale. Figures that dwarf galaxies. Space as an active participant, not empty background
- **Thick confident ink lines** — bold black outlines of varying weight, heavier on foreground figures and leading edges, thinner on background elements. Hand-inked energy with slight imperfection, not digital smoothness
- **Visible halftone dot patterns** — Ben-Day dots in magenta, cyan, and yellow visible in color areas, as if printed on a 1970s offset press. The mechanical texture of cheap newsprint reproduction
- **Dynamic diagonal compositions** — nothing horizontal, nothing static. Every figure lunging, every machine angled, every energy bolt cutting diagonally across the frame. The ground itself tilts
- **Collage textures in cosmic backgrounds** — Kirby pioneered photo-collage in comics, pasting photographic textures into cosmic scenes and energy fields. Abstract photographic texture fragments (crumpled foil, magnified circuits, chemical reactions) composited into painted/inked cosmic vistas
- **Heroic upward angles** — camera placed low, looking up at figures that tower above the viewer. Even seated figures feel monumental through perspective
- **Massive hands and fingers** — hands drawn twice their anatomical proportion, fingers thick as sausages, gripping cosmic weapons or clenching into planet-sized fists. Hands are characters in themselves

## Compositional Patterns

| Content Structure | Composition | Reference |
|-------------------|-------------|-----------|
| Hierarchy/levels | Colossal godlike figure towering at the top, normal-sized figures dwarfed below, energy crackling between the scales — the Celestial looming over Earth | Galactus consuming a planet, humans as ants below |
| Flow/process | Explosive diagonal thrust from lower-left to upper-right, each stage an escalating burst of energy, fists punching through sequential barriers | An action sequence of a hero smashing through obstacles in sequence |
| Connections | Kirby Krackle energy fields connecting figures across the composition, cosmic power flowing between entities through arcing dot-cluster channels | The Boom Tube — energy portal connecting two worlds |
| Cycles | Cosmic wheel of energy, figures arranged around a central power source with Krackle crackling at the hub | The Source Wall — figures embedded in circular cosmic structure |
| Comparison | Two colossal figures facing off, fists raised, energy crackling between them, the composition split by a diagonal line of Kirby Krackle | Thor vs. Galactus — gods in opposition across a cosmic divide |
| Categories | Each category as a different cosmic entity or piece of alien technology, each with its own energy signature color and Krackle pattern | The New Gods — each character defined by unique cosmic power visual |
| Scale | Extreme size contrasts — galaxy in background, planet in midground, cosmic being in foreground, human figure as a speck | The Celestials — beings so large that planets are pebbles |
| Power/impact | Central starburst explosion with force rings radiating outward, debris and Kirby Krackle flying from the impact point | A cosmic punch landing — the moment of maximum power |

## Typography

- Bold condensed sans-serif — chunky, heavy, superhero comics lettering
- ALL CAPS always — lowercase does not exist in Kirby's universe
- Sound effects as massive starburst lettering: BOOOM!, KRAKK!, DOOM!
- Caption boxes: Yellow or white rectangles with bold black text, dramatic narrative voice
- Title lettering: Giant, filling the top of the composition, sometimes warped or exploding
- Text can crack, shatter, or radiate energy lines — letters as physical objects being hit by cosmic force
- Black outlines around all lettering, white fill, occasionally colored for emphasis
- NO delicate fonts, NO thin weights, NO decorative scripts — everything is BOLD and LOUD

## Anti-Patterns

- **NOT Golden Age Comics** — Golden Age (1940s-50s) is simpler, more static, brighter primary colors, less extreme foreshortening. Kirby is specifically the LATER cosmic period (1960s-70s Fourth World, Eternals, Cosmic Marvel). The energy, the scale, the Krackle, the impossible foreshortening — these are what separate Kirby from generic vintage comics. If it looks like a 1940s Superman cover, it is WRONG.
- **NOT modern digital comics** — No digital gradient rendering, no photorealistic shading, no smooth airbrushed color. This must look hand-inked with visible halftone dots, as if printed on cheap 1970s newsprint. The mechanical texture of offset printing IS the aesthetic.
- **NOT generic superhero** — Generic superheroes stand in neat poses with clean anatomy. Kirby anatomy is SQUARED OFF, BLOCKY, MONUMENTAL — shoulders like cinder blocks, fists like bricks, jaws like anvils. If the figure looks like it was drawn by a modern digital artist, it is wrong.
- **NOT without Kirby Krackle** — The swirling black dot-cluster energy fields are THE signature element. Without Kirby Krackle, it is just a comic book, not Jack Kirby. Every cosmic scene, every energy blast, every portal MUST include these characteristic clusters of irregular black dots.
- **NOT small or intimate** — Everything in Kirby's universe is COSMIC SCALE. If a scene feels personal, quiet, or human-sized, it is missing the point. Even a conversation between two people should feel like gods negotiating the fate of the universe.
- **NOT Keith Haring or Pop Art** — Though both use bold outlines and flat color, Haring is flat simplified figures and Kirby is hyper-detailed cosmic machinery. Though both use dots, Kirby Krackle is clustered energy dots, not Ben-Day halftone patterns used decoratively.
- **NOT Bauhaus** — Bauhaus is systematic, harmonious geometric abstraction. Kirby is explosive, chaotic, narrative-driven cosmic energy. The dots are organic clusters, not geometric precision.
- This must look like the universe is splitting open and a cosmic god is stepping through, crackling with energy, fist raised to reshape reality — not a clean superhero illustration that happens to have some dots.

Epic narratives, technology showcases, power/impact messaging, cosmic scale comparisons, energy and transformation topics, breakthrough announcements, confrontation/competition framing, anything that needs to feel like the most important event in the history of the universe.
