# rinpa

The decorative genius of 17th-century Japanese art elevated to transcendent beauty — Ogata Kōrin's red and white plum blossoms against a gold river, Tawaraya Sōtatsu's wind and thunder gods on gold leaf, Sakai Hōitsu's summer and autumn grasses. Bold graphic simplification meets lavish material luxury. Irises reduced to pure rhythm, waves stylized into perfect patterns, gold leaf backgrounds that glow with inner light. This is nature transformed into symbol, seasonal beauty made eternal through radical design choices. Asymmetric compositions, dramatic cropping, and the courage to leave vast areas of gold or silver absolutely empty.

## Color Palette

- Primary: Deep indigo (#1A237E), crimson red (#DC143C), rich green (#228B22), gold (#FFD700)
- Ground: Gold leaf (#FFD700), silver leaf (#C0C0C0), warm cream (#FFFEF0)
- Accents: Pure white (#FFFFFF), black ink (#000000), soft pink (#FFB7C5)
- Seasonal: Cherry blossom pink (#FFB7C5), autumn red (#B22222), spring green (#00FF7F)
- Water: Silver-blue (#708090), stylized wave patterns, the famous Rinpa water motif
- Natural pigments: Mineral-based colors with matte intensity, not glossy

## Visual Elements

- **Gold leaf backgrounds** — luminous gold ground that glows, not flat yellow paint
- **Silver leaf accents** — moon, water, clouds rendered in actual silver
- **Bold graphic simplification** — natural forms reduced to essential curves and shapes
- **Asymmetric composition** — dramatic off-center balance, weight shifted to one side
- **Radical cropping** — flowers, trees, figures cut by frame edge, suggesting continuation
- **Vast empty space** — large areas of gold or ground left untouched, silence as design
- **Tarashikomi technique** — wet-on-wet ink creating soft organic edges
- **Repeating rhythms** — patterns of leaves, waves, petals creating visual music
- **Flat color planes** — no Western-style shading, forms defined by outline and flat fill
- **Seasonal symbols** — cherry blossoms, plum, iris, maple, chrysanthemum, pine
- **Stylized water** — the famous Rinpa wave pattern, rhythmic and geometric
- **Cloud bands** — gold or silver clouds floating through compositions

## Typography

- **Japanese calligraphy** — brush-written characters as art elements
- **Waka poetry** — integrated verse in the Heian courtly style
- **Seal stamps** — artist's marks as compositional elements
- **Minimal text** — Rinpa relies on visual communication, text is sparse
- **Period-appropriate** — historical Japanese scripts only
- **Integrated placement** — text placed within the composition, not separate
- **Gold ink on dark** — metallic writing on deep backgrounds for luxury

## Anti-Patterns

- **NOT realistic** — nature is stylized, graphic, symbolic — never photographic
- **NOT symmetrical** — asymmetry is fundamental, avoid centered balance
- **NOT cluttered** — empty space is essential, not something to fill
- **NOT Western perspective** — flat picture plane, no vanishing points
- **NOT muted** — colors are rich and saturated against gold/silver grounds
- **NOT minimalist** — decorative richness balanced with restraint, not stripped bare
- **NOT busy patterns** — rhythmic repetition, not random complexity
- **NOT Chinese painting** — specifically Japanese aesthetic, Rinpa school conventions
- This must look like Ogata Kōrin or Sakai Hōitsu created it — bold graphic nature on luminous gold, dramatically cropped, asymmetrically balanced, with vast empty spaces that sing.
