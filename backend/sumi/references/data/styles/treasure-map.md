# treasure-map

X marks the spot on aged parchment — the romance of pirate adventures and buried gold, sea monsters lurking in unmapped waters, dotted trails leading to hidden riches. Burnt edges and coffee stains on paper that looks five hundred years old, compass roses pointing to mysterious destinations, ships with billowing sails approaching dangerous shores. Handwritten labels in archaic scripts, "Here Be Dragons" warnings, and the promise that somewhere at the end of this winding path lies something wonderful. This is the map as story, geography as adventure, the wayfinding document that doubles as a promise of excitement.

## Color Palette

- Background: Aged parchment (#F5DEB3), burnt yellow (#DEB887), tea-stained cream (#FAEBD7)
- Lines: Sepia ink (#704214), brown-black (#3D2B1F), faded black (#4A4A4A)
- Water: Aged teal (#5F9EA0), antique blue (#6B8E9E), or simply parchment with wave patterns
- Land: Tan (#D2B48C), warm brown (#A0522D), green for forests (#6B8E23)
- Accents: Red X marks (#8B0000), gold (#FFD700) for treasure, dotted trail in contrasting color
- Creatures: Illustrated in ink colors, sea monsters and mythical beasts
- Text: Sepia or brown-black, hand-lettered, archaic spellings

## Visual Elements

- **Aged parchment texture** — stained, wrinkled, torn edges, water damage, burn marks
- **X marks the spot** — the iconic red or dark X indicating treasure location
- **Dotted trail** — path showing the route to follow, sequential waypoints
- **Compass rose** — ornate directional indicator, often with decorative flourishes
- **Sea monsters** — serpents, krakens, whales in the ocean areas
- **Ships** — period sailing vessels, sometimes with flags
- **Mountains** — simple triangular or hatched peaks indicating terrain
- **Trees** — stylized forest indicators, often small repeated symbols
- **Buildings** — simple structures marking towns or landmarks
- **Coastlines** — irregular, hand-drawn shore lines with wave patterns
- **Banners and cartouches** — decorative title frames, distance scales
- **Handwritten labels** — place names in archaic or decorative script
- **Warnings** — "Here Be Dragons," "Danger," skull and crossbones
- **Scale and legend** — sometimes included, often deliberately absent

## Typography

- **Hand-lettered** — archaic, slightly irregular, quill-pen aesthetic
- **Blackletter hints** — gothic influences for titles, medieval feeling
- **Italic scripts** — calligraphic place names, decorative flourishes
- **Archaic spellings** — "Ye Olde," "Iland," period-appropriate orthography
- **Decorative initials** — large ornate first letters for titles
- **Banner text** — words on flowing ribbon banners
- **Mixed sizes** — important locations larger, minor features smaller
- **Compass labels** — N, S, E, W in decorative style

## Anti-Patterns

- **NOT clean or precise** — the aged, imperfect quality is essential
- **NOT modern** — no contemporary design sensibilities, no clean minimalism
- **NOT digital-looking** — must feel hand-drawn on physical material
- **NOT geographically accurate** — stylized terrain, not satellite imagery
- **NOT too legible** — some mystery, some areas unclear, invites exploration
- **NOT sterile** — texture, wear, damage, history visible
- **NOT colorful** — limited palette, sepia tones, aged colors only
- **NOT realistic illustration** — stylized symbols and conventions
- This must look like an actual aged treasure map — parchment texture, sepia ink, hand-drawn coast lines, mysterious destinations, and the promise of adventure at the X.

## Best For
