# treasure-map

Weathered parchment unfurled to reveal hand-drawn lands and seas — ink coastlines bordered by lurking sea monsters, mountains rendered as clusters of peaked triangles, dotted trails winding toward a bold red X marking buried treasure. Compass roses bloom in corners, decorative cartouches frame the title in flourishing script, and "Here Be Dragons" warns of unknown territories. This is the romance of Age of Exploration cartography: part navigation tool, part storytelling artifact, entirely adventure.

## Visual Elements

- Aged parchment background with uneven coloration, darker toward edges, lighter areas where worn
- Torn, burnt, or ragged edges suggesting the map has survived adventures
- Coastlines drawn with feathered ink lines, thicker on land side, hatched shading into water
- Mountains shown as clusters of inverted V shapes with shading on one side (the "molehill" convention)
- Forests as masses of small circular tree symbols clustered together
- Rivers as winding double lines tapering upstream
- Dotted trail (X---X---X) marking the path to follow, often with numbered waypoints
- Large decorative compass rose with ornate N/S/E/W points, radiating direction lines
- Sea monsters (serpents, kraken, whales) emerging from ocean waves
- Sailing ships drawn in profile, sails full, positioned on the sea
- "X marks the spot" — bold red X at treasure location, often with radiating emphasis lines
- Decorative cartouche (ornate frame) containing map title in flourishing script
- Scroll banners with location names: "The Cursed Swamp", "Dragon's Peak"
- Warning text in old script: "Here Be Dragons", "Mare Incognitum"
- Coordinate grid lines or radiating rhumb lines from compass points
- Small illustrations at points of interest: skull, crossed bones, anchor, crown, castle icon
- Foxing spots (brown aging marks) and fold creases on the parchment
- Hand-drawn quality — intentionally imperfect lines, ink pooling at line intersections

## Typography

- Titles: Ornate blackletter or decorative script in cartouche frame — "Ye Olde Map of..."
- Location names: Hand-lettered italic script following coastline curves or terrain features
- Warnings: All-caps sepia text with archaic spelling — "HEERE BE DRAGONS"
- Labels: Slightly uneven hand-drawn lettering, not perfectly aligned
- Banner scrolls containing important text, ribbon borders
- Compass directions (N, S, E, W) in decorative capitals with serifs and flourishes
- Scale bar labeled in leagues or nautical miles with old-style numerals
- All text appears hand-inked with slight variations in line weight
- Latin phrases for authenticity: "Mare Incognitum", "Terra Nullius"

## Anti-Patterns

- **NOT clean digital map** — Treasure maps are hand-drawn artifacts with imperfections. No vector precision, no perfect circles, no computer-generated coastlines.
- **NOT modern cartography** — No satellite imagery aesthetic, no topographic contour lines, no GPS coordinate systems. This is pre-scientific romance.
- **NOT minimalist** — Treasure maps are decorated with monsters, ships, compass roses, and cartouches. Empty ocean should have wave patterns or sea creatures.
- **NOT Google Maps style** — No road networks, no pin markers, no flat UI aesthetic. This is illustrated storytelling, not navigation app.
- **NOT dark or grimdark** — Despite pirates and monsters, this is adventure romantic, not horror. Warm parchment tones, exciting not frightening.
- **NOT perfectly preserved** — The map should look aged, handled, weathered. New-looking parchment breaks the spell. Add stains, tears, fold lines.
- **NOT historically accurate** — This is the fantasy of old maps, not actual historical accuracy. Sea monsters belong here even though real cartographers rarely drew them.
- **NOT text-heavy** — Let the visual geography dominate. Labels should be sparse and hand-lettered, not paragraphs of information.
- This must look like a prop from a pirate adventure — aged parchment, mysterious markings, decorative flourishes, promising secrets and treasure — not a clean informational map with old-timey fonts.
