# tibetan-thangka

Sacred geometry meets luminous mineral pigment — the devotional painting tradition of Tibetan Buddhism. Figures seated on lotus thrones within concentric aureoles of gold and rainbow light, mandalas built from nested squares and circles in turquoise, vermillion, and deep lapis blue, all rendered with the meticulous line work and saturated mineral color of paintings on cotton canvas. This is spiritual architecture made visible: every element placed with iconographic precision, every color carrying symbolic weight, every border a gateway between realms.

## Visual Elements

- Concentric aureoles radiating outward from central subjects — rings of rainbow light in red, orange, gold, green, blue, each ring a thin precise band
- Lotus thrones rendered as layered petal platforms — each petal individually outlined in gold with alternating pink and white fills
- Fine gold line work tracing every contour — not thick outlines but delicate gilded lines that define forms and add luminosity
- Mineral pigment saturation — colors appear dense, opaque, and matte like crushed stone ground into paint, not digital or glossy
- Mandala geometry: nested squares rotated 45 degrees within circles, with elaborate gate structures (torana) at north/south/east/west
- Cloud scrolls in stylized curlicue forms — clouds rendered as tight spiral bands of blue and white, never fluffy or realistic
- Flame aureoles (vesica-shaped) surrounding wrathful figures — tongues of flame in red, orange, and gold radiating symmetrically
- Tiny detailed figures arranged in rows along borders and in corner vignettes — each one complete with robes, halos, and hand gestures (mudras)
- Textile brocade border patterns framing the composition — geometric repeated patterns in gold and deep colors simulating silk mounting
- Lotus flowers and eight auspicious symbols (parasol, golden fish, treasure vase, lotus, conch shell, endless knot, victory banner, dharma wheel) as decorative motifs
- Backgrounds of deep solid color — no texture simulation, just dense saturated mineral pigment ground
- Symmetrical compositions with bilateral mirror balance — left mirrors right around a central axis
- NO photorealistic rendering, NO soft atmospheric perspective, NO loose brushwork
- NO Western perspective — space is hierarchical (important = larger, higher) not optical

## Typography

- Titles rendered in Tibetan-inspired decorative script — angular letterforms with horizontal head-strokes, suggesting Uchen script character
- Gold text on deep indigo or burgundy cartouches — rectangular label panels with ornamental border trim
- Text labels placed within brocade-bordered panels below or beside subjects
- Seal-like circular emblems containing single characters or symbols as punctuation marks
- No modern sans-serif fonts — all text appears hand-lettered in a formal calligraphic tradition
- Smaller labels in clean white or gold on colored ground, larger titles in elaborate gilded script
- Text integrated into the composition as part of the devotional architecture, not floating freely

## Anti-Patterns

- **NOT ukiyo-e** — Thangka uses CONCENTRIC SYMMETRY and HIERARCHICAL SCALE, not asymmetric woodblock-print compositions. NO wave patterns, NO Edo period subject matter, NO bokashi gradients
- **NOT rinpa** — Despite gold usage, thangka gold is FINE LINE WORK and SOLID GROUND, not hammered leaf texture. NO tarashikomi pooling, NO scattered petals on gold, NO decorative flatness-for-its-own-sake
- **NOT art-nouveau** — Thangka curves are GEOMETRIC and PRECISE (circles, arcs, measured proportions), not organic whiplash curves. NO flowing tendrils, NO sinuous asymmetry
- **NOT mandala coloring book** — This is DENSE, SATURATED, FULLY PAINTED with mineral pigments, not outlined shapes waiting to be filled. NO thin black outlines on white, NO empty spaces
- **NOT psychedelic poster art** — Despite rainbow concentric rings, thangka is ORDERED, SYMMETRICAL, and PRECISE, not melting or distorted. NO optical illusion patterns, NO warp effects
- **NOT generic Buddhist clip-art** — Figures have SPECIFIC ICONOGRAPHIC DETAIL (mudras, attributes, throne types), not simplified cartoon Buddhas. NO smiling simple Buddhas, NO zen circle minimalism
- **NOT sumi-e** — Thangka is FULLY SATURATED COLOR on COLORED GROUND, not monochrome ink wash. NO empty space meditation, NO visible brush texture, NO mist
- This must look like a devotional painting from a Tibetan monastery — meticulously detailed, jewel-like mineral colors glowing against deep indigo, sacred geometry organizing every element — not a simplified spiritual illustration or new-age poster
